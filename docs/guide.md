# Modryn Studio — User Guide

Internal AI company OS. Assign work to AI team members, run strategy threads, log decisions.

---

## First launch

1. Enter your name and an optional company description.
2. Create a project — everything (conversations, tasks, decisions) lives inside a project.

You will not see these screens again unless the database is cleared.

---

## Projects

All your work is scoped to a project. Switch, rename, or delete from the dropdown at the top of the sidebar.

- **Switch** — click the project name, then click any row.
- **Rename** — hover the row, click the pencil icon, type a new name, press Enter.
- **Delete** — hover the row, click the trash icon, type the project name to confirm.
- **New project** — click **+ New project** at the bottom of the dropdown.

---

## DM — one-on-one chat

Click a team member in the sidebar to start a conversation.

- Type and press **Enter** to send (desktop). On mobile, use the send button.
- Attach a file with the paperclip icon — its contents are sent with your message.
- Hover an AI message (desktop) to copy it, retry it, or log a decision/org fact.
- **Synthesize** (document icon, top-right on desktop / strip above input on mobile) — extracts decisions and tasks from the conversation. Review each proposal, confirm or dismiss.

---

## Threads — brief the whole team

1. Click **New Thread**.
2. Enter an optional title and your brief.
3. Pick a sequence type (Strategy, Technical, Design, etc.).
4. Optionally exclude members (eye icon) or drag to reorder.
5. Send — members respond one at a time in sequence.

After all members respond, use **Synthesize** (top-right) to extract decisions and tasks.

---

## Tasks — assign work to a member

1. Click **New Task**, enter a title, optional description, and pick a member.
2. Click **Play** on a task card to run it — the member produces a structured deliverable.
3. Click the chevron on any card to read the output.

---

## Other surfaces

- **Inbox** — UI built, no messages yet. Member-initiated async messages are not implemented.
- **Calendar** — placeholder only, not built.
- **Reddit** — paste a Reddit URL to fetch and format the thread for use in a conversation.
